01 Abstract

10–40% of genetic test results are classified as Variants of Uncertain Significance (VUS) — mutations whose impact on health is unknown. These non-actionable results leave patients in diagnostic limbo and disproportionately affect underrepresented populations.

GeneSage uses Evo2, a 7B-parameter DNA foundation model trained on 9.3 trillion nucleotides across all domains of life, to predict whether a genetic variant is pathogenic or benign — with zero-shot accuracy exceeding 90%.

02 Aim

To build an accessible web platform that democratizes AI-powered genomic variant analysis — enabling researchers and clinicians to predict the impact of DNA mutations without GPU infrastructure or bioinformatics expertise.

🧬 For Clinicians

Rapidly triage VUS results before expensive functional follow-up. Get AI-powered predictions for any SNV in seconds.

🔬 For Researchers

Screen candidate variants, validate against ClinVar, and explore gene sequences with an interactive genomic browser.

Threshold + confidence scoring

Δ = score(variant) − score(reference)  │  Δ < −0.000918 → Pathogenic  │  Δ ≥ −0.000918 → Benign

Threshold Derivation

ROC analysis on 500 BRCA1 variants. Optimal boundary via Youden's J statistic (max TPR − FPR).

Confidence Metric

Normalized distance from threshold: |Δ − threshold| / σ, capped at 100%.
